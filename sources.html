Sources.

Every material property, comparison and demonstrated result on this site traces to the peer-reviewed literature below. Diamond devices today are currently limited by doping and wafer-scale manufacturing, not by these intrinsic material limits. That is the problem Kara exists to solve.

Material properties

  1. C. J. H. Wort & R. S. Balmer, "Diamond as an electronic material."Materials Today 11 (1–2), 22–28 (2008) — bandgap, breakdown field, thermal conductivity, saturation velocity, figures of merit
  2. J. Isberg et al., "High carrier mobility in single-crystal plasma-deposited diamond."Science 297, 1670–1672 (2002) — electron and hole mobility in single-crystal CVD diamond
  3. T. R. Anthony et al., "Thermal diffusivity of isotopically enriched ¹²C diamond."Physical Review B 42, 1104 (1990) — enhanced thermal conductivity of isotopically purified diamond

Quantum

  1. M. H. Abobeih et al., "One-second coherence for a single electron spin coupled to a multi-qubit nuclear-spin environment."Nature Communications 9, 2552 (2018) — T₂ = 1.58 s NV coherence in ¹²C diamond
  2. B. Hensen et al., "Loophole-free Bell inequality violation using electron spins separated by 1.3 kilometres."Nature 526, 682–686 (2015) — entanglement between NV centers across 1.3 km
  3. M. Pompili et al., "Realization of a multinode quantum network of remote solid-state qubits."Science 372, 259–264 (2021) — three-node diamond quantum network
  4. T. Wolf et al., "Subpicotesla diamond magnetometry."Physical Review X 5, 041001 (2015) — 0.9 pT/√Hz NV magnetometer sensitivity
  5. A. Sipahigil et al., "An integrated diamond nanophotonics platform for quantum-optical networks."Science 354, 847–850 (2016) — color centers coupled to on-chip diamond cavities

Thermal & power

  1. J. W. Pomeroy et al., "Low thermal resistance GaN-on-diamond transistors characterized by three-dimensional Raman thermography mapping."Applied Physics Letters 104, 083513 (2014) — GaN-on-diamond thermal performance
  2. H. Umezawa, "Recent advances in diamond power semiconductor devices."Materials Science in Semiconductor Processing 78, 147–156 (2018) — diamond Schottky diodes, high-voltage blocking
  3. J. Achard et al., "High quality MPACVD diamond single crystal growth."Journal of Physics D: Applied Physics 40, 6175 (2007) — single-crystal CVD growth
